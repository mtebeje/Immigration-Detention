Immigration Detention Locations in the US

Mapped Based on ICE Data FY23

December 17th, 2023

The Department of Homeland Security (DHS) manages the largest immigration detention system in the world and spends more on immigration enforcement than on all other federal enforcement agencies combined.

According to Immigration and Customs Enforcement they reported over 36,000 individuals are currently in ICE detention according to data as recently as December 3, 2023. Of those individuals almost 68% have no criminal record and a majority of migrants have been held due to minor offenses, including traffic violations. In November alone ICE and CBP detained and booked over 17,000 people.

Migrants stand near the border wall after crossing the Rio Bravo river with the intention of turning themselves in to the U.S. Border Patrol agents, as seen from Ciudad Juarez, Mexico April 14, 2023. REUTERS - Jose Luis Gonzalez

According to ICE data, over 90 percent of people are held in privately-run immigrant detention centers. And although the number of detain migrants are slowly decreasing ICE and private prison companies are continuing to expand electronic monitoring. ICE Alternatives to Detention (ATD) or ecarceration programs are currently monitoring 191,212 families and single individuals as reported by ICE.
